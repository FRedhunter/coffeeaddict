House Blend Coffee

House Blend coffee is special because each coffee shop, roaster, and drinker has their own unique take on it. The creation and serving of these blends truly reflect the preferences of the roaster or distributor. For instance, Starbucks refers to their House Blend as the first blend they ever crafted back in 1971, saying it embodies their identity and the quality of coffee they aim to provide. The brewing method also plays a crucial role in how the flavors and aromas come through in the final cup. Different styles of brewing cater to individual tastes, but some have distinct advantages. A popular choice for House Blend coffee is the Chemex machine, which is known for enhancing the smooth texture and pure flavor of the blend. This brewing method takes a “slow-and-steady” approach, much like a drip machine, but it uses a filter that’s three times thicker. The Chemex maintains the ideal brewing temperature of 190 degrees, removing excess oil from the beans and resulting in a clean, flavorful cup. Coffee brewing can truly be seen as an art form, where every detail matters—from water temperature to the characteristics of the beans. Crafting a delicious cup of House Blend requires time and effort, from harvesting and roasting to tasting and refining the perfect blend that ends up in your mug.

French Roast

The French Roast style of coffee originated in France in the 1800s but is now a globally recognized roasting method. While different roasters have their unique takes, French Roast is known for its bold, caramelized flavor without the bitterness or acidity often found in other dark roasts. Flavor notes can include chocolate, smoke, and roasted nuts, creating a rich and full-bodied coffee experience. To achieve this, beans are roasted at temperatures between 450 and 455 degrees for an extended period, resulting in a dark brown color and a light oil sheen. Once the desired roast is achieved, the beans are quickly cooled to stop the process. Whether you grind the beans or keep them whole, you can brew your French Roast coffee using your preferred method.

Columbian Roast

Colombian coffee is a standout favorite among coffee enthusiasts. Renowned for its exceptional beans, Colombia's tropical climate, high altitude, and rich soil provide the ideal growing conditions. The country benefits from abundant rainfall and mild temperatures, resulting in a unique flavor profile and strong aroma. Coffee is grown across the northern regions of Colombia, notably in departments like Cauca, Caldas, and Risaralda. Key varieties include Caturra, Typica, and Castillo, among others. A significant aspect of Colombian coffee is that about 600,000 growers handpick the beans, ensuring only the ripest are selected. Most Colombian coffees are wet-processed, contributing to their vibrant acidity. This acidity is bright and refreshing, not to be confused with sourness. The balanced flavor profile of Colombian coffee—often featuring notes of caramel, nuts, and chocolate—makes it versatile for espresso and blends. With a variety of flavors, from fruity berries to floral notes, Colombian coffee truly offers something for everyone, solidifying its status as a crowd-pleaser.
